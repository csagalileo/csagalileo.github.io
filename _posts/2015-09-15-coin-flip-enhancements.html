Here are some creative projects to enhance the CoinFlip app and help build your programming skills.

  1. Modify the app so that the user can shake the phone to flip the coin. (HINT: Use the Accelerometer Sensor.)
  2. Modify your app so that “heads” or “tails” is spoken when the coin is flipped. (HINT: Use the TextToSpeech component.)
  3. Modify the event handler in the Coin Flip app to use random fraction instead of random integer. (HINT: A random fraction is a decimal number between 0 and 1, not including 1. Some examples: 0, 0.25, 0.33, 0.5, 0.66, 0,75, 0.99.)
  4. Advanced: According to this report, if you stand a bunch of Lincoln pennies on their edge and then bang the table, they have a strong bias toward coming up heads. Let’s suppose the coin has a 70% chance of coming up heads (30% tails) in this experiment. Create a model to simulate this biased coin. (HINT: Create a separate project for this problem.)
  5. Advanced: You now have an app that can flip a two-sided coin. Modify your app that so that it can flip a three-sided coin. (Hint: You will need an if/else block with three conditions. You’ll need a third image for this problem; here’s one that is openly licensed: coin on edge.)
  6. Be Creative. Come up with your own ideas to enhance your app.

Complete the reflection on Google Classroom and submit your .apk file. To get the .apk file from App Inventor, package your app by selecting Build and downloading the .apk to your computer.
