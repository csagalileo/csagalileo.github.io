Part 1

A program passes The Turing Test (en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turing_Test) if a person can have a conversation with both it and a person and not be able to tell which one is the computer.

You will be talking with some chatterbots. Here are some sample questions you can ask the chatterbots.

  1. Are you a computer?
  2. What is the next number in the sequence 3, 6, 9, 12, 15?
  3. What do you think of nuclear weapons?
  4. What is 2 x 78?
  5. What is the square root of two?
  6. Add 34957 to 70764.
  7. Do you like school?
  8. Do you like dancing?
  9. What day is it today?
  10. What time is it?
  11. How many days are there in February in a leap year?
  12. How many days are there in a week?
  13. For which country is the flag a red circle on a white background?
  14. Do you like to read books?
  15. What food do you like to eat?

Try each of these chatterbots and ask it a few of these questions. Talk to each machine until it says something that no human, not even a human pretending to be a machine, would be likely to say. Explain why no person would ever give this response. What might the machine have been doing to produce that response?

  • Eliza (http://www-ai.ijs.si/eliza/eliza.html)
  • Santa (http://www.alicebot.org/Santa/)
  • Elbot (http://www.elbot.com/)
  • Bearbot (http://www.square-bear.co.uk/games/bearbot/chat.htm)
  • Mitsuku (http://www.square-bear.co.uk/mitsuku/chat.htm)
  • Cleverbot (http://www.cleverbot.com/)

Try to come up with one diagnostic question that could help you to differentiate human and machines. Ask this question to all 6 of these bots. How well did they do? Does the machine always respond the same way when asked the same thing?

In your journal, also answer the following questions:

  • How realistic are each of the chatterbots? Which ones would pass the Turing Test?
  • Which of the chatterbots was the most like a real person? Why/how?
  • Which questions did you ask to help you diagnose whether or not the chatterbot is a computer or not? Which questions were most effective?
  • Can these machines think? Why or why not? If this machine passes the Turing test, what can we conclude about its intelligence?
